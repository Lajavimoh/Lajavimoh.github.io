The Problem

Small business owners are losing leads every day

Most Jimdo users run service-based businesses: yoga teachers, coaches, consultants, photographers. They have a website but no real system for capturing or following up with enquiries. A generic contact form is not enough.

Two core problems: Forms are generic and collect the wrong information, and even when leads arrive, there is no system to manage, prioritise, or act on them intelligently.
❓
Wrong questions, wrong leads
Users copy basic templates. They collect name and email but miss the signals that tell them who is a serious lead.
🖺
Follow-up is manual and inconsistent
Users respond from their personal inbox, forget to follow up, and lose visibility after the first reply.
🎯
No sense of lead quality
Every enquiry is treated the same. There is no way to know who is ready to buy versus who is just browsing.
⏱
Time is the biggest constraint
Small business owners wear every hat. They need systems that surface the right action at the right moment.

Vision CRM
Capturing a lead is only half the job. What does the user do with enquiries once they arrive? This is where we extended the product into an AI-powered CRM layer.
The Insight

Small business owners don't need a Salesforce. They need a system that tells them what to do next.

The Vision CRM builds on the leads Smart Forms captures. Every submission flows into a dashboard where the platform does the analysis, scoring leads, surfacing actions, and generating suggested replies, so the owner can focus on doing the work, not managing the pipeline.

Beta Learnings

What the data taught us

Three learnings from the beta that shaped how we thought about AI transparency and product simplicity.

01
Smart suggestions need to feel earned, not random
Users trusted recommendations more when they could see why the suggestion was made. Showing the reasoning increased follow-through significantly.
02
The two products are deeply connected
Lead scoring only works if the form asks the right questions. A weak form produces weak signals. Strong form design is the foundation of useful CRM intelligence.
03
Small business owners act on simplicity
The CRM features that got used most were the ones with a single clear action button. Complexity killed engagement. Every screen needs one obvious next step.

Reflection

What this project taught me

💡

The hardest design problem is not the UI; it is knowing which information to surface and when. Lead scoring and smart suggestions only feel useful when the timing is right. Showing the right thing five minutes too late feels as bad as not showing it at all.

🤝

Smart features need to be humble. Suggestions, not commands. Confidence with an escape hatch. Every recommendation in the CRM was designed to feel like a smart colleague, not an automation that decided things without you.

🔗

Two products designed as a system are always stronger than two designed in isolation. Smart Forms and Vision CRM only reached their full potential when we stopped treating them as separate features and started thinking about the complete lead lifecycle.
